VI. Modern Developments & Future Directions

1. High-Dimensional Testing

  • Large p, small n problems: more variables than observations
  • Regularization methods: LASSO, Ridge, Elastic Net
  • False Discovery Rate: Benjamini-Yekutieli, adaptive methods
  • Factor modeling & Principal Factor Approximation
  • Permutation methods: Westfall-Young for dependent tests

2. Machine Learning Approaches

  • Model comparison via cross-validation: k-fold, leave-one-out, bootstrap
  • Information criteria: AIC, BIC, DIC, WAIC
  • Ensemble methods: Random Forests (variable importance), Gradient Boosting (partial dependence)
  • Deep learning: SHAP values, Integrated Gradients, permutation feature importance

3. Causal Inference Tests

  • Potential Outcomes Framework: Y(1), Y(0) under treatment/control
  • Balance and overlap tests after matching/weighting
  • Exclusion restriction & Instrumental Variables tests
  • Difference-in-Differences: checks parallel trends
  • Regression Discontinuity: McCrary test for manipulation

4. Reproducibility & Replicability

  • Adjustments for p-hacking: two-stage testing, holdout samples
  • Pre-registration: confirmatory vs exploratory studies
  • p-curve analysis: detect selective reporting
  • Bayesian checks: prior predictive p-values, posterior predictive checks
  • Multiverse analysis: robustness across analytic choices

5. Key Takeaways

  • Modern statistical practice blends classical methods with computational and ML approaches
  • High-dimensional and dependent data require advanced methods
  • Reproducibility, transparency, and causal inference are central in contemporary research
  • Effect sizes and practical significance complement traditional hypothesis testing
