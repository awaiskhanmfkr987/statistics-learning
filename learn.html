1. Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics summarize data using numerical measures such as mean, median, variance, and standard deviation.

2. Parametric Tests

  • One-sample t-test: Tests whether a sample mean differs from a known population mean.
  • Independent t-test: Compares means of two independent groups.
  • Paired t-test: Compares means of the same group before and after treatment.
  • ANOVA: Compares means of three or more groups.
  • Pearson Correlation: Measures linear relationship between two continuous variables.

3. Non-Parametric Tests

  • Mann-Whitney U: Alternative to independent t-test.
  • Wilcoxon Signed Rank: Alternative to paired t-test.
  • Kruskal-Wallis: Alternative to one-way ANOVA.
  • Spearman Correlation: Rank-based correlation method.

4. Categorical / Distribution Tests

  • Chi-Square Test: Association between categorical variables.
  • Binomial Test: Tests proportion of success/failure.
  • Sign Test: Tests median difference.
  • Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test: Tests distributional fit.
