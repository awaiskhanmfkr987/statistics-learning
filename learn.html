1. Z-Tests

Used primarily for large sample sizes (n>30) or when the population variance is known.

One-Sample Z-Test: Compares a sample mean to a known population mean to determine if there is a significant difference.

Two-Sample Z-Test: Compares the means of two independent samples to determine whether they differ significantly.

Z-Proportion Test: Compares the population proportions to test if they differ from each other.

2. Studentâ€™s T-Tests

Used for smaller sample sizes or when the population variance is unknown.

One-Sample T-Test: Compares a sample mean to a hypothesized value.

Independent (Two-Sample) T-Test: Compares the means of two distinct independent groups.

Paired (Dependent) T-Test: Compares means from the same group measured at different times or under different conditions.

Welchâ€™s T-Test: A variation of the independent t-test used when equal variance assumption (homoscedasticity) is violated.

3. Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)

Used to compare the means of three or more groups.

One-Way ANOVA: Compares means across three or more groups based on one factor.

Two-Way ANOVA: Compares means based on two independent factors and examines interaction between them.

Repeated Measures ANOVA: Used when the same subjects are measured multiple times under different conditions.

MANOVA (Multivariate ANOVA): Extension of ANOVA for multiple dependent variables.

ANCOVA (Analysis of Covariance): Combines ANOVA and regression to control for effects of continuous covariates.

4. Correlation and Regression

Pearsonâ€™s Correlation (r): Measures strength and direction of linear relationship between two continuous variables.

Linear Regression: Models the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables; can be used for prediction and interpretation of associations.

5. Other Parametric Tests

F-Test: Used to compare variances of two populations; also forms the basis for ANOVA.

Chow Test: Used in econometrics to determine if coefficients in two linear regressions on different datasets are equal.
