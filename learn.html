1. Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive Statistics are used to summarize, organize, and describe the main features of a dataset. They do not make predictions or generalizations beyond the data.

Mean: The arithmetic average of all observations. It represents the central value of the data.

Median: The middle value when data is arranged in ascending or descending order. It is robust to outliers.

Variance: Measures how far observations spread from the mean by averaging squared deviations.

Standard Deviation: The square root of variance. It indicates the average distance of observations from the mean.

2. Parametric Tests

Parametric tests are statistical tests that assume the data follows a specific distribution, usually the normal distribution, and that the data is measured on an interval or ratio scale.

One-Sample t-Test:
This test is used to determine whether the mean of a single sample is significantly different from a known or hypothesized population mean.

Independent t-Test:
This test compares the means of two independent groups to determine whether there is a statistically significant difference between them.

Paired t-Test:
This test compares two related measurements taken from the same individuals, such as before and after treatment.

ANOVA (Analysis of Variance):
ANOVA is used to compare the means of three or more independent groups to determine whether at least one group mean differs significantly from the others.

Pearson Correlation:
This test measures the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two continuous variables. The correlation coefficient ranges from âˆ’1 to +1.

4. Categorical / Distribution Tests

These tests are used for categorical data or to examine whether data follows a specific probability distribution.

Chi-Square Test:
This test examines whether there is a significant association or independence between two categorical variables.

Binomial Test:
This test evaluates whether the observed proportion of successes differs significantly from a theoretical proportion.

Sign Test:
This non-parametric test evaluates whether the median of a population differs from a specified value using only the direction of differences.

This test compares an observed distribution with a theoretical distribution to determine whether they differ significantly.
